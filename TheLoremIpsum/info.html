The buttons at the top change which section of the menu is shown in the options directly underneath them

Then you can look through the selections underneath, to see what choices are available in that section. The price for the currently shown item is shown next to the add button. Tapping the add button adds the item to your list. The list is directly below the add button where it says "your list".

You can remove items from your list, by adjusting amounts with the minus symbol button for that row. When you change the quantity of an item to zero, a box will pop-up checking if you want to take the item off of your list.

Items are shown in the list in the order you added them, first item at the top - though if you add an item that is identical to something already in your list, the quantity for that item will increase by 1 rather than adding another line to your list.

Websites and apps are made from a bunch of computer files, lovingly typed and drawn by humans, in a process known as "Software Development". Some of these files tell the device what to show. Like many websites and apps, TeaRounder uses HTML files (.html) for general layouts, CSS files (.css) to set how things are styled and JavaScript files (.js) to give instructions for what happens in the web browser when users click or tap something.
